# Cover email — draft for sending the legal positioning letter to Anthropic

Suggested recipient: developer-relations@anthropic.com (or whichever direct contact you have from prior conversations).

---

**Subject:** Pre-launch courtesy notification — commercial Cowork skill (Cloud2GroundAI)

Hello,

I'm an independent developer launching a small commercial macOS product called Cloud2GroundAI under Carlano Technology Solutions LLC. The product installs a Cowork skill that lets Claude delegate mechanical work to a locally-installed IBM Granite model, plus a small offline chat window for the user to keep working when they have no internet. Users continue to use their own Claude Desktop / CLI / Cowork install for the cloud side — Cloud2GroundAI never makes calls to the Anthropic API itself.

I wanted to make sure the architecture and business model are consistent with your Consumer Terms of Service, your February 2026 stance on third-party Claude harnesses, and Cowork plugin policies before going public with the v1.0 free preview (planned for early July). The attached document describes how Cloud2GroundAI works, explains why it sits outside the harness category, and asks a few specific questions in Section 7 about commercial Cowork-skill distribution that I'd love your perspective on.

Happy to provide source, sample traffic logs, or anything else that would help your review. I'd rather get this right up front than launch and find out I should have raised it first.

Thanks for your time,

Andrew Carlile
Carlano Technology Solutions LLC
carlanotech@pm.me

---

## Notes on the draft

- Reads as a courtesy / good-faith heads-up rather than a request for permission, which is the right register — you're not asking them to bless you, you're showing them the architecture and inviting dialogue.
- Mentions the attachment and points specifically at Section 7 for the questions, so they know where to land if they want to respond in detail.
- Closing sentence ("rather get this right up front than launch and find out I should have raised it first") signals you're acting in good faith and have a real launch timeline.
- About 180 words — short enough to be read, long enough to land the context.
